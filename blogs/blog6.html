Deprivation and Trials

I remember, and I can never forget, on shab-e-Qadr I stayed up all night. I was weeping throughout the night. Not asking for mercy, not asking for ilm, not asking for peace. Instead a list of material tarnish things. But whatever I wished, indeed it was granted.
I had made a list of ten items, which I thought were the most important in my life, yes they were but to an extent. Starting the list with beauty, something that I had never felt, but I came to know later that how you look doesn't matter at all! The second thing that I wished for was respect, I am sure there are many more lessons that I have got to learn so.... I don't remember the others but one of them definetely was Compassion and love, and I had put a condition to that; Make them love me more than I love them, because I really never felt loved.
The wish was indeed granted
After all why not! It was shab-e-Qadr! And oh my! Allah was indeed showering his mercy on all of us. Boy, Did I taste love!
So sweet, just like a hump of sugar, starts to melt in your mouth. Feels like an endless fountain. The depth of world, the warmth of sun, so undescribable. You can only feel it, it cannot be expressed. A tentative estimate!
So would you keep that hump of sugar forever in your mouth? I would, if only the sugar was forever to stay! Correct me on this one if I'm wrong, is the sugar everlasting? NO! it is the taste that is everlasting! You only need the sugar when you want it in dish, say for example you want to make halwa, and you don't add any sugar to it, how will it taste? You will definetely feel an urge to add sugar to your halwa because you are accustomed to eating halwa sweet!
Similarly, we can conclude the same for human memories: The people leave, but memories stay.

As Aqib Shah says:
"No light could ever replace her splendour
she is brilliant all along
Sometimes
She would be the sunset
lighting up the horizon
with orange hue,
and
Sometimes
she would become
the night sky
only to give
her Sun to the one
it rains on"

But all along, I failed to realise one simple thing, I had a compassionate friend watching my back all along, Al-Hay Al-Haq, Ya-Raheem.
What have I been tested with? Loss? seperation? Guilt? And what is that compared to what our messengers endured? Nuh AS invited his people for 900 years! nobody paid heed to him. Yaqub AS, lost his dear son, and for years he cried and cried until he lost his sight, and his own sons were the ones who had betrayed him.Yusuf AS was tested with betrayal by his own brothers, husn, and Lust! Muhammad SAW were also tested, and Allah was the one to test him! Wasn't He a piece from Allah's noor? Then why was he tested? Because Allah loved him and wanted to make an example out of him. So that we may learn and thrive. Because with hardship comes ease and with more hardship comes more ease. And it is known that Allah only tests those whom he loves!
So if anything is edurable, I simply endure it speechlessly. Because its an honor to be tested!
